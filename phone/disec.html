DISEC

 

Organ Description

The Disarmament and International Security Committee is the first committee of the general assembly which deals with disarmament, global challenges and threats to peace that affect the international community and seeks out solutions to the challenges in the international security regime. Formed in 1945, the DISEC considers all deactivation of arms/weapons and international security matters within the scope of the United Nations Charter or relating to the powers and functions of any other organ of the United Nations

DISEC was formed to respond to the need for an international forum to discuss issues of peace and security among members of the international community. According to the UN Charter, the purpose of DISEC in the General Assembly is to establish ‘general principles of cooperation in the maintenance of international peace and security, including the principles governing disarmament and the regulation of armaments and also to give ‘recommendations with regard to such principles to the members or to the Security Council.’

 

Agenda: Regulating Production, Collection and Trade of Small Arms and Light Weapons

Any portable weapon designed for individual use classifies as a small arm and/or light weapon. This includes revolvers, self-loading guns, pistols, rifles, carbines, sub-machine guns and light machine guns among a few. Over the two last two decades their use has increased exponentially and so has their illicit circulation. This has led to violent armed conflicts and proxy wars in several regions of the world. Another important aspect is the acquisition of these arms by non-state actors and civilians which resulted in gruesome human rights violations and disruption of international security.

Weighing in on the issue, the UN has taken substantive measures and introduced treaties such as the ATT, ITI, PoA etc. but their implementation has been difficult due to loopholes. Analysing this issue is paramount as it increasingly poses the worst global security threat. The committee aims at addressing this problem in detail and building consensus in order to form stricter policies that help combat repercussions generated from the use of SALW.
