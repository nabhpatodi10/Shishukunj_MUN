Organ Description

The Generalitat of Catalonia is the law making institution of Catalonia and consists of two bodies: The Executive Council and The Parliament. In the committee, both the bodies have to work coherently and make decisions. The Executive Council consists of Executive Councillors (those who have jurisdiction over a government department), and Non - Government Councillors (those who hold non-government positions outside of the Council). The Parliament consists of Parliament Councillors who are leaders of the three major political parties (PDeCAT, ERC and CUP). Agenda: Catalan Independence Referendum, 2017

Catalonia is one of the seventeen autonomous communities that constitute the Kingdom of Spain. In August 2006, the Catalan Statute of Autonomy was amended, which provided the Generalitat of Catalonia with more control over its domestic affairs. However soon after 2010, the Spanish Constitution struck down most of the 2006 changes. For centuries, Catalans were content to be a part of Spain with the said privileges given to them, but the 2010 ruling once again raised the demand for independence.

The committee is set in June 2017, just after the official announcement of the Catalonian Government to hold an Independence Referendum. It is to decide how Catalonia will function as an independent nation and plan for the referendum to be held in October, while tackling all interference by Spain.
