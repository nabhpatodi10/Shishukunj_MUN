Organ Description

The United Nations Commission on Science and Technology for Development is a subsidiary body of the Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC). It was established in 1992 as a result of the restructuring and revitalization of the United Nations in the economic, social and related fields.

The Commission provides the General Assembly and ECOSOC with high-level advice on relevant issues through analysis and appropriate policy recommendations or options in order to enable those organs to guide the future work of the United Nations, develop common policies and agree on appropriate actions.

The Commission acts as a forum for:

  • The examination of science and technology questions and their implications for development; the advancement of understanding on science and technology policies, particularly in respect of developing countries.
  • The formulation of recommendations and guidelines on science and technology matters within the United Nations system.

 

Agenda: Regulation of Cryptocurrency Along With its Impact on Global Inflation

A Cryptocurrency is a type of a digital asset or a financial instrument based on a network that is distributed across a large number of computers meant to execute payments or transactions. While, the world has seen privately owned cryptocurrencies, the advent of government regulated Central Bank Digital Currencies which work on the same blockchain technology has necessitated reforms for both the kinds of currency. The use of cryptocurrency is widespread; while it is used in money, laundering it is also legal tender in El Salvador. Such widespread use also come with environmental as well as economic concerns. Cryptocurrency has been a contributor in both global warming, and the world semiconductor shortage. We would like all delegates to discuss debate upon the judicious and efficient use of cryptocurrencies.
