A Message from the Chairperson

Dear delegates,

It is my pleasure to welcome you all to the 6th iteration of the Shishukunj Model United Nations. I am Bhavya Modi, and I'm honoured to serve as the Chairperson for the International Monetary Fund.

The agenda of this committee addresses one of the most crucial problems faced by the economies- hyperinflation. The ongoing hyperinflation in Venezuela is considered as one of the worst hyperinflation ever occurred and has led to severe socio economic and political turmoil in the country. It is integral for you all to look at the situation from all possible angles and perspectives. If you are well researched, it will help your participation and boost up your confidence in the committee.

During the course of the conference, we expect you all to have substantive discussions and come up with comprehensive solutions. The ultimate goal of the committee is to reach a consensus.

I as well as the other bureau members will try to make sure that the conference is a memorable and a learning experience for all the delegates. I look forward to meeting you all soon.

Regards,

Bhavya Modi

International Monetary Fund

 

Organ Description

The International Monetary Fund, commonly known as the IMF is an international financial institution with a current strength of 190 member nations. The aim of the IMF is to foster global monetary cooperation, secure financial stability, facilitate international trade, promote high employment, reduce poverty around the world and most importantly, prevent international financial crises and assist in recovering from them. The IMF receives most of its funds from its member nations in the form of Quotas. Member nations that are experiencing shortages/ facing adversities related to balance of payments can borrow money from the IMF.

 

Agenda: Hyperinflation with Special Emphasis on Venezuela

At present, the biggest crisis faced by the Venezuelans in their day-to-day lives is hyperinflation. This economic catastrophe refers to currency instability that began in Venezuela, in 2014 and has worsened over the years. Under President Nicolas Maduro, the entire economy collapsed. With the inflation rates rising rapidly, the country surpassed others with an annual inflation rate of 1,000,000%.The Humanitarian, health and economic challenges in Venezuela have worsened significantly due to the ongoing political turmoil.

Hyperinflation cannot be arrested overnight once it has started. The council must reach a consensus to ensure the economic stability as well as the well-being of the citizens and the economy. The diplomats are expected to come up with an upgraded and more comprehensive set of regulations which could be implemented in Venezuela with reference to the ongoing social, economic and political turmoil going on in the country.
