All India Political Party Meet

 

Organ Description

The All India Political Party Meet (AIPPM) serves as an influential supplementary committee to the parliament. It serves as a forum for political discussion which couldn’t take place in the parliament due to time constraints. The committee allows the diverse political groups of India to reach a conclusion on decisions of national importance. Taking into consideration that India has a multi-party system, different political groups bring in their diverse perspectives and opinions on various important issues. The committee is expected to formulate a final document which will serve as a recommendation to the government.

 

Agenda: Evaluating  The Existing Election Setup With Special Emphasis On One Nation One Election

Elections vest power upon citizens to choose their government. Elections are perhaps the most vital organ of democracy. Holding elections in world’s second most populous country isn’t an easy task.

With developments in and around India, modifying method of holding elections is required. India, a three-tier federal system, following multi-party form of government, practices a complex electoral system having own flaws that need rectification. A reformed electoral policy is needed to keep up with pace of development and overcome drawbacks of the existing electoral system.

One Nation One Election is a concept helping achieve an effective and efficient electoral setup  by holding simultaneous elections for all levels of a federation.

It has been under discussions and controversies in India since 1983. Some parties desire its implementation while few demand modifications. Our question here is, if applied in India, whether it’ll help overcoming limitations of existing system and increase the pace of development?
