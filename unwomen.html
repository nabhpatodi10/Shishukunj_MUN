A Message from the Chairperson

Dear delegates,

It gives me immense pleasure to welcome you to Shishukunj MUN 2021. I, Tamanna Chhabra, will be serving  as the Chairperson of United Nations Entity for Gender Equality and the Empowerment of Women (UNWOMEN).

I am a 12th grader studying Humanities with Applied Mathematics and Psychology. I have been participating in MUNs since grade 7, and I am grateful for how they have helped me grow as an individual. As a chairperson, my advice to you is to confidently  represent your respective countries, while respecting your fellow delegates.

One of the oldest and most spread sources of inequality has been through gender based discrimination. Women, especially, have encountered obstacles in every aspect of life. The committee will mainly focus on discussion of these hinderances spread across various dimensions, which women face in their path of growth and development.

Lastly, I’d like to emphasize that the most important thing for you is to learn and have fun. Please feel free to contact me for any queries or assistance that you might need.

Hope to see you at the conference!

Regards,

Tamanna Chhabra

UNWOMEN

 

Organ Description

UN Women -or United Nations Entity for Gender Equality and the Empowerment of Women- was created in July 2010 by the United Nations General Assembly by merging of four distinct UN bodies which were focusing on women’s empowerment. Its main purpose is divided in five prioritized areas which are: support women’s leadership participation, combat violence against women, ensure the participation of women in all peace and security processes, empower women economically, and make sure the inclusion of gender equality to national agendas. Generally, it functions in a parallel manner with other UN bodies and tries to ameliorate their actions in terms of gender equality. More specifically, UN Women assists inter-governmental bodies with their work, provides Member States with technical and financial support when they request it, and -lastly- give guidance to other UN bodies in order to sustain their accountability towards gender sensitive issues. It consists of 41 Member States which are elected for a three-year term and the latter is distributed geographically. Current executive director is Phumzile Mlambo-Ngcuka from South Africa.

 

Agenda: Achieving SDG 5 Gender Equality and Empowerment of All Women and Girls

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development to promote gender equality and empower all women and girls not only focuses on ending discrimination against women in all forms but also eradicating stereotypes which the society has created and ensuring that women are not segregated from various activities on the bases of their gender. Women and girls cannot achieve their full potential if they are denied anything which is available to men.

Even though there has been progress; we still have a long way to go. Women are still under-represented  as voters and men are still preferred as leaders in politics, 129 million girls are out of schools all over the world that is 1/4th of the total girl population, women are discriminated against in their workplaces, are provided less wages, and are compared to their male counterparts.

Right to education, equal employment and political opportunities are some of the issues that our committee will be focusing on. It is very important for delegates to deliberate and discuss these situations and come up with sustainable and long term solutions so that  we can  help women from every stratum of society stand on their own and steer their lives as per their own wishes and so that no women or girl ever will have to face any gender based inequality anywhere.
